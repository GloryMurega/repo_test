2. Data category(ies), purpose(s) and legal basis for processing your personal data

In the context of this study “AI at the Workplace” conducted online on oTree via Prolific, only anonymous survey data will be processed. In individual cases, it cannot be ruled out that natural persons can be identified by their own information or by a combination thereof. In these cases, this data protection information applies.

Processed data categories are personal data entered by you yourself as part of the study questionnaire. These are anonymized (blackened/deleted) before the evaluation begins.

Steps of data processing are: 1)Provision of invitation link via Prolific, 2)temporary online survey in oTree, 3)anonymization of any personal data available, and 4)evaluation.

Notes: The online survey is conducted on Prolific and oTree. Technical participation data (e.g., IP address etc.) is not collected. Any presentations of the evaluation results are made exclusively with anonymized data.

The purpose of the processing is research insights on AI-Human collaboration implemented as part of doctoral research at the University of Paderborn 2025 (via online survey with Prolific).

If you are identifiable, the legal basis for the processing of your personal data in the context of carrying out the above-mentioned processing activity is your consent in accordance with Art. 6 para. 1 subpara. 1 lit. a) GDPR.

4. Duration of storage of your personal data
If and to the extent that the processing of your personal data is based on your consent, your personal data will only be stored until you revoke your declaration of consent, unless there is also another legal basis for the processing (Art. 17 para. 1 lit. b) GDPR). If individuals are identifiable, personal data will be deleted / anonymized within six months after the end of the survey, no later than 12 months (December 2026). And if the data is available in the form of paper documents – destroyed.
5. Rights of data subjects
As a data subject, you may at any time exercise the rights granted to you by the GDPR; These are:
  • the right to information as to whether and which of your data is being processed in accordance with Art. 15 GDPR, § 12 DSG NRW;
  • the right to request the correction or completion of data concerning you in accordance with Art. 16 GDPR;
  • the right to erasure of data concerning you in accordance with Art. 17 GDPR, § 10 DSG NRW;
  • the right to restrict the processing of data in accordance with Art. 18 GDPR;
  • the right to data transfer of data concerning you in accordance with Art. 20 GDPR.
6. Revocability of your declaration of consent
Any consent given can be revoked in whole or in part at any time without giving reasons. The revocation does not affect the lawfulness of the processing carried out on the basis of the consent before the revocation (Art. 7 para. 3 GDPR). As a result, we may no longer continue the data processing based on this consent for the future and must delete your data, unless there is also another legal basis for the processing (Art. 17 para. 1 lit. b) GDPR). If you would like to revoke your declaration of consent in whole or in part, please contact the contact person mentioned above or send an email to: datenschutz@uni-paderborn.de
Please note that it is no longer possible to delete your data and, if necessary, to change and view your personal data after six month after the end of the survey (by december 2026 at the latest) due to the anonymisation of your data.
7. Right to lodge a complaint
In addition to the above-mentioned rights, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority under data protection law (Art. 77 GDPR) if you believe that the processing of personal data concerning you violates these data protection requirements; e.g. at the University of Paderborn responsible State Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information North Rhine-Westphalia, Kavalleriestr. 2-4, 40213 Düsseldorf, Phone: 0211/38424-0, E-Mail: poststelle@ldi.nrw.de
